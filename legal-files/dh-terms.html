    • BUIDL Collections—this is a tool that can be used to create BUIDLs.
    • BUIDL Reports—this is a feature that uses artificial intelligence like ChatGPT to create content and reports.
    • BUIDLink—this is a feature that allows venture capital investors to connect with VC teams.

  6. Bounties—hackers, developers and other users can create and participate in bounty programs and get rewards by completing tasks and winning competitions (“Bounty”).
    • DAO Bounties—users can solve problems and get rewarded via cryptocurrencies.
    • Bug Bounty—users can find bugs/risks in a project, smart contract, or any software repository and get incentives after identifying and/or solving the bugs/risks.
    • Mini Bounties - users can complete tasks and get rewarded. Tasks are designed and managed by bounty owners.
  7. Grants—developers can submit projects, participate in grant funding and related community governance rounds such as quadratic funding via donation from users or other hackers/developers, and receive funds to be distributed based on voting results (“Grant”). Users can donate to the project that will generally have a bigger funding pool and matching pool than other Grants.
    • Community Funding—Single grant rounds funding a specific set of BUIDLs or projects. The grant round distributes grant funding with any self-defined voting mechanisms such as quadratic voting, 1-person-1-vote, token voting, or zero-knowledge based secure voting.
    • Grant DAO—A Grant DAO is a dedicated, often long-term program to continuously support early-stage ventures and public good projects. A Grant DAO can apply any funding and governance mechanisms to support BUIDL projects related to a certain technology, mission, blockchain ecosystem, or problem.
  8. MACI—this is a special voting mechanism for promotional purposes. Developers can set this up to allow users to vote or donate to a project. Developers cannot see the vote; they just see final results. The results are validated by technologies such as zero-knowledge proofs.
  9. Ideas—developers can post ideas using hashtag “#Your_ideathon”, and also can get involved in other developers’ ideas.
  10. Ideaism—AI generated ideas posted on DoraHacks, in parallel with human posted ideas. Human users and AI can interact with each other around a specific idea on DoraHacks.
  11. Mini Grant—organizations can provide financial support to early-stage hacker teams with innovative ideas and projects that hold promise for the future and the society. Through the Mini Grant platform, teams can submit their BUIDL projects and receive funding.
  12. Mail—this is a convenient way that allows users to send in-site mails to the target users.
